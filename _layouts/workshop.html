Next-Generation Robot Navigation: Learning, Reasoning, and Adaptation{%- else -%}{{ site.title }}{%- endif %}

{{ page.subtitle }}

This workshop will delve into the future of robotic systems, focusing on advancements in navigation powered by emerging technologies like world models, foundation models, and generalist robotics. We aim to bring together a diverse audience from academia and industry to discuss challenges and opportunities in integrating these approaches across various domains of robotics. The workshop will emphasize cross-disciplinary insights, fostering innovation toward intelligent, adaptable, and scalable robotic systems for diverse real-world applications. Topics include but are not limited to (see Call for Papers for more details):

  • Foundation models and world models for robotic perception, planning and control
  • Large-scale learning and adaptation techniques for generalizable robot navigation
  • Uncertainty-aware navigation in complex, dynamic environments
  • Integration of semantic understanding and reasoning for intelligent navigation
  • Novel architectures and methods for multi-modal sensor fusion and scene understanding
  • Benchmarking and evaluation of generalist navigation approaches across diverse scenarios
