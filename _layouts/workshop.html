Recent advances in foundation models, world models, and large language models have shown remarkable potential in enhancing robot perception, reasoning, and decision-making capabilities. This workshop aims to explore how these emerging technologies can be effectively integrated into robot navigation systems to achieve more robust, adaptable, and generalizable behaviors. We will focus on the following key aspects:

  1. Learning-based Navigation
  2. Foundation Models in Navigation
  3. Robot Exploration and Active Mapping
  4. World Models for Reasoning
  5. Multi-robot Navigation
Through invited talks, paper presentations, and panel discussions, we will bring together researchers from robotics, computer vision, and machine learning communities to address critical challenges in scaling robot navigation capabilities across diverse environments and tasks. Topics include but are not limited to (see Call for Papers for more details):

  1. Foundation models and world models for robotic perception, planning and control
  2. Large-scale learning and adaptation techniques for generalizable robot navigation
  3. Uncertainty-aware navigation in complex, dynamic environments
  4. Integration of semantic understanding and reasoning for intelligent navigation
  5. Novel architectures and methods for multi-modal sensor fusion and scene understanding
  6. Benchmarking and evaluation of generalist navigation approaches across diverse scenarios
